Emotional Intelligence

Cultural insights

    Definition
  • IQ , known as intelligence quotient is a measure of someones intelligence often found by special tests.
  • EQ , known as Emotional intelligence is the ability to use , manage and understand your own emotions in positive ways.
EQ values that are generally spoken about. Self-awareness: The ability to recognize and understand your own emotions, including their impact on your thoughts and behavior. Self-awareness also involves recognizing your strengths and limitations. Self-regulation: The ability to manage your emotions, impulses, and reactions. This includes controlling negative emotions such as anger or frustration and adapting to changing circumstances. Social skills: The ability to interact effectively with others, including communication skills, empathy, and conflict resolution. Socially skilled individuals are able to build and maintain positive relationships. Empathy: The ability to understand and share the feelings of others. Empathy allows you to connect with others on an emotional level and respond appropriately to their emotions. Motivation: The drive to pursue goals with energy and persistence. Motivated individuals are often more resilient in the face of setbacks and more likely to achieve success. Devloping emotional intelligence can lead to many benefits , including improvement in all of the above.
